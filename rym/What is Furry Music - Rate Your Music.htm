What is "Furry Music"?

A list by GoodnightGirl

[List825393] | +75

Visibility: public private

Furry music is anything that wants to call itself "furry music". It sounds arbitrary, sure, but there are many artists that use their furry identity as the catalyst to creation. I try to find the unique voice and sounds that these artists have. I'm always looking for the most outstanding, unique, undoubtedly furry music

All albums on this list incorporate at least one of the following:
- Cover art with an anthropomorphic animal
- Song titles or lyrics that describe the furry community, or come from a more "animal" perspective
- Accompanying art featuring furries or fursuits as the artist's image (twitter, youtube, personal site)
- Anything else legal and ethical that only furry musicians would do compared to non-furry musicians

Suggestions welcome. Keep in mind, this list is meant to shine a light on places in the community that could use it.

This list is forever in progress. Not all write-ups will be of equal quality, but I'll update them from time-to-time. Rarely will I remove someone from the list entirely.

Gears of the Apparatus

Cutting Teeth (2011)


Cutting Teeth is a breakcore/chiptune album that stands out from the incredibly saturated line of breakcore/chiptune furry artists. It's found a place in my heart for its layers and nuances to keep things constantly moving, making something useful and engaging out of the noise it creates. I am a big fan of the guitar-solo-esque melodies dabbled here and there for every track.

My only gripe with this album is the final track, which is a remix of Renard's Post Traumatic Stress Disorder. The harmonic complexity is lost, but the rest of the album is absolutely worth a look.

https://gearsoftheapparatus.bandcamp.com/album/cutting-teeth

The Furry Music Conundrum

One more thought (unrelated to its placement in the list) before the list ends:

A lot of furries seem to talk about furry music. I've seen furries ask why they don't see many furry musicians, without a clue of where to find more. I've seen furries write-off "furry music" as an arbitrary label that gets in the way of music being good. There have been dozens of furries who've tried to unify furry musicians into compilations: https://efmc.bandcamp.com/ or communities: https://www.furaffinity.net/user/furrymusicians, with incredibly varied success rates. FurryMusicians maybe being the only one that's had consistent success. But no matter your perspective, there are already many artists who deliberately put their furriness into their creative works, we might as well put a label on it and see what happens.

I feel like furry music is the epitome of a niche that can never get enough of itself. There's always going to be people wanting to express their furry identity as something forward-facing, and to surround themselves with people who share that vision, to go as far as to listen to music that affirms their feelings about the world and their place in it. But it doesn't have to be outspoken either, there's plenty of subtle furryisms too, where artists don't show their furry face in their music at all unless you bother checking their Twitter or ask them privately. There are listeners who definitely also express their furriness in this same way too.

Anyways, I'm always really lenient on how to define it. Anything other than complete openness would mean missing out on some amazing artists I've stumbled into thanks to them belonging to this niche that keeps popping up in different places on the internet for some reason.
